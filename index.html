About

I am a Senior Lecturer and Distinguished Associate Research Fellow at Stirling College, Chengdu University (CDU), specializing in Computer Vision, Explainable AI, and Deep Learning Applications. My research addresses critical challenges in image segmentation, infrastructure inspection automation, and medical image analysis.

I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Technology from Southwest Jiaotong University (China, 2021), an M.Tech. in Artificial Intelligence from the University of Hyderabad (India, 2015), and a B.Sc. in Computer Engineering from Aleppo University (Syria, 2011). My professional experience includes roles as Postdoctoral Research Fellow and Lecturer at Southwest Jiaotong University and the SWJTU-LEEDS Joint School.

My research focuses on developing multi-scale segmentation architectures and hybrid deep learning frameworks that achieve robust performance with minimal labeled data. Through innovative attention mechanisms and iterative refinement techniques, I bridge advanced AI methodologies with practical applications in infrastructure monitoring and medical diagnostics.

Research Interests

Computer Vision

Advanced image processing techniques, semantic segmentation, and object detection with applications in infrastructure monitoring and medical imaging.

Explainable AI (XAI)

Developing interpretable machine learning models that provide transparent decision-making processes for critical applications.

Deep Learning Applications

Novel architectures and training methodologies for real-world problems, particularly in weakly supervised scenarios.

Image Segmentation

Advanced segmentation techniques for medical images, infrastructure inspection, and general computer vision tasks.

AI for Infrastructure

Automated inspection systems for pavement crack detection, bridge monitoring, and civil engineering applications.

Medical Image Analysis

AI-powered diagnostic tools for medical imaging, including lesion detection and organ segmentation.
