About

I am a Distinguished Associate Research Fellow at Stirling College, Chengdu University (CDU), specializing in Computer Vision, Explainable AI (XAI), and Deep Learning Applications. My research focuses on developing innovative solutions for image segmentation, AI for infrastructure inspection, and medical image analysis.

With a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Technology from Southwest Jiaotong University (2021), I have established myself as a leading researcher in AI and computer vision applications. My work bridges theoretical advances with practical applications, particularly in pavement crack detection and medical image analysis.

As Principal Investigator of China's National Foreign Experts Programme for Young Foreign Talents, I lead cutting-edge research in developing cost-effective crack segmentation systems. My innovations have resulted in two granted patents and have attracted significant funding from national and international sources.

I am passionate about making AI more interpretable and accessible, with a particular focus on weakly supervised learning techniques that can work with minimal labeled data - a crucial advancement for real-world applications.
